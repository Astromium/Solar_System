The Sun

The Sun (or Sol), is the star at the centre of our solar system and is responsible for the Earth’s climate and weather. , its so heavy , its gravity controles the entire solar system.

Age : 4.6 Billion Years

Type : Yellow Dwarf

Temperature : 5500° C

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and due to its proximity it is not easily seen except during twilight.

Diameter : 4879 Km

Distance from the Sun : 57,91 million km

Temperature : -173 To 423 °C

Venus

Venus is the brightest planet in the Solar System and can be seen even in daylight if you know where to look. it is called the morning star and the evening star.

Diameter : 12.104 Km

Distance from the Sun : 108.2 million km

Temperature : 462 °C

Earth

Well this planet dont need any description actually , its our lovely , beautiful and unique home to mankind and all the living beings.

Diameter : 12756 Km

Distance from the Sun : 149.6 million km

Temperature : -88 To 55 °C

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is the second smallest planet in the solar system. , Mars is also often described as the “Red Planet” due to its reddish appearance . and it could be our next home.

Diameter : 6.792 Km

Distance from the Sun : 227.9 million km

Temperature : -153 To 20 °C

Jupiter

The planet Jupiter is the fifth planet out from the Sun, and is two and a half times more massive than all the other planets in the solar system combined. It is made primarily of gases and is therefore known as a “gas giant”

Diameter : 142.984 Km

Distance from the Sun : 778.3 million km

Temperature : -142°C

Saturn

This is the jewel in the solar system's crown , its beauty is beyond belief , although saturn is a gas giant but yet it is so light that it can float on water.

Diameter : 120,536 Km

Distance from the Sun : 1,42 billion km

Temperature : -178°C

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. While being visible to the naked eye, it was not recognised as a planet due to its dimness and slow orbit. it is also a gas giant, and guess what it rains diamond inside it!

Diameter : 51,118 Km

Distance from the Sun : 2,87 billion km

Temperature : -216 °C

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun making it the most distant in the solar system. it is a giant ball of methane gas and it was discovered by pure mathematics and calculations (thanks to orbital mehanics).

Diameter : 49,528 Km

Distance from the Sun : 4,49 billion km

Temperature : -214 °C

Pluto

Well I know Pluto is not considered as a planet , but how can you ignore this little boy , Pluto was first seen by use of a telescope in 1930. Like Uranus and Neptune, Pluto can not be seen by the naked eye.

Diameter : 2372 km

Distance from the Sun : 5.91 billion km

Temperature : -223 °C
