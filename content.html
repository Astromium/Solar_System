1. The Sun

The Sun (or Sol), is the star at the centre of our solar system and is responsible for the Earth’s climate and weather. , its so heavy , its gravity controles the entire solar system.

2. Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and due to its proximity it is not easily seen except during twilight.

3. Venus

Venus is the brightest planet in the Solar System and can be seen even in daylight if you know where to look. it is called the morning star and the evening star.

4. Earth

Well this planet dont need any description actually , its our lovely , beautiful and unique home to mankind and all the living beings , so we have to take care of it before its too late :(

5. Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is the second smallest planet in the solar system. , Mars is also often described as the “Red Planet” due to its reddish appearance . and it could be our next home.

6. Jupiter

The planet Jupiter is the fifth planet out from the Sun, and is two and a half times more massive than all the other planets in the solar system combined. It is made primarily of gases and is therefore known as a “gas giant”

7. Saturn

This is the jewel in the solar system's crown , its beauty is beyond belief , although saturn is a gas giant but yet it is so light that it can float on water.

8. Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. While being visible to the naked eye, it was not recognised as a planet due to its dimness and slow orbit. it is also a gas giant, and guess what it rains diamond inside it!

9. Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun making it the most distant in the solar system. it is a giant ball of methane gas and it was discovered by pure mathematics and calculations (thanks to orbital mehanics). Learn more about its discovery here
